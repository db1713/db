User Experience Research Case Study

Background:
Students use Spotify almost everyday to get them through their jam packed life juggling assignments, collaborative projects, sports and social life. Spotify hosts a plethora of music which helps them be relaxed, beat the nerves, make music the life of a party etc.
It is important for them to be able to find music quickly, share playlists and manage their listening experience.

Motivation:
This study was driven by Spotify's heavy use among college students for studying, workouts, and socializing. Despite its popularity, user interviews uncovered usability issues with playlist management, music discovery, and social features. The study aimed to identify these pain points and suggest solutions to make Spotify more user-friendly for students.

ROLE & RESEARCH GOALS

Role

My role involved multiple responsibilities:

  • User Interviews: Conducted interviews to identify Spotify’s usability issues among students.
  • Task Identification: Helped define evaluation tasks focused on playlist management, social features, and music discovery.
  • Data Collection and Analysis: Organized think-aloud sessions, tracked metrics, and used statistical measures to analyze usability.
  • Reporting and Recommendations: Documented findings, calculated SUS scores, and suggested design improvements.

Research Goals

  1. Evaluate User Experience: Assess how well students interact with Spotify’s features, focusing on playlist management, social interactions, and the "Now Playing" screen.
  2. Identify Usability Issues: Pinpoint pain points and challenges through task completion rates, error rates, and other metrics.
  3. Measure User Satisfaction: Use SUS scores to evaluate usability against industry benchmarks.
  4. Provide Actionable Recommendations: Propose design changes to improve Spotify’s user experience for students.

METHODS AND DATA ANALYSIS TECHNIQUES

Recruitment and Participant Demographics

The study recruited 12 participants, consisting of both local and international students from various academic programs, aged between 17 and 26. Participants were selected to represent typical Spotify users, with varying levels of experience using the platform, from three to 11 years. Recruitment occurred through university channels and word-of-mouth.

User Interviews and Observations

The study began with user interviews to understand participants’ experiences and uncover potential usability issues related to playlist management, music discovery, and social features. The interviews guided the identification of three key tasks:

  1. Adding a song to a playlist from the “Now Playing” screen.
  2. Navigating to a friend’s profile and adding a song from their playlist.
  3. Grouping two songs together in a playlist.
A think-aloud protocol was used during the tasks, where participants verbalized their thoughts while completing each task. This provided insights into their decision-making process and any challenges they faced.

Confidence Intervals

A confidence interval is like a range that tells us where we think the true value is likely to be. Imagine you measured something, like the average height of students in a class, and you got an average of 5 feet 6 inches. Instead of saying “the average height is exactly 5 feet 6 inches,” you say, “I’m pretty sure the true average is between 5 feet 4 inches and 5 feet 8 inches.”

System Usability Scale (SUS)

Administered to measure overall user satisfaction, with scores compared against the industry benchmark of 70. Statistical tests were used to evaluate whether the mean SUS score significantly differed from the benchmark.

Findings:

  • Average SUS: 53.13
  • Standard Deviation: 17.84
  • T-Value: -3.27. This t-value is much smaller than the critical value (1.80), and it is negative, meaning the sample mean is far below 70.
  • We can be 99% confident that this application has an average score lesser than the industry score of 70.
  • There is not enough evidence to conclude that this interface's perceived usability is high, with a mean SUS score greater than 70. In fact, the mean SUS score of 53.13 is significantly lower than 70, indicating that the usability might need improvement.
  • Confidence Interval: 43.85 to 62.39

KEY FINDINGS, REFLECTIONS - TAKEWAYS & DESIGN RECOMMENDATIONS

Key Findings

1. Navigation Challenges: Many participants struggled to navigate to their friend’s profile and view their music activity. The process required multiple steps, making it unnecessarily complex. Searching for friends was difficult because it required navigating through the user’s own profile, which was not intuitive.

2. Playlist Management Issues: Managing playlists, including rearranging songs and grouping them from the same album, was confusing for most users. The edit functionality was hidden under settings, which was not where users expected to find it. Users frequently tried to use the "+" button on the "Now Playing" screen to add songs to a playlist, but it added them to the "Liked Songs" list instead, leading to confusion.

3. User Satisfaction: The System Usability Scale (SUS) scores were below the industry benchmark of 70, with an average score of 53.13. This indicates a generally low perceived usability for Spotify’s interface, especially concerning playlist management and social navigation. A confidence interval analysis confirmed that the actual usability score was likely to be significantly lower than the benchmark, showing room for improvement.

4. Consistency Across Platforms: Users noted inconsistencies between Spotify’s mobile and desktop interfaces. The desktop version appeared cluttered, while some mobile features were less accessible.

Takeaways

1. Complex Social Features: The process of finding and interacting with friends' music activities is not intuitive, leading users to need assistance or give up. Simplifying social features could enhance user engagement.

2. Poor Playlist Management Experience: Users struggled with organizing and managing their playlists. This impacted overall satisfaction, with tasks like grouping songs and rearranging playlists being especially challenging.

3. Low Perceived Usability: With SUS scores indicating frustration, it’s clear that Spotify’s interface does not currently meet user expectations for ease of use, especially in terms of finding friends, managing playlists, and adding songs.

4. User Expectations vs. Reality: There is a disconnect between what users expect certain features to do (e.g., the "+" button) and their actual functionality. Addressing these mismatches could reduce user confusion.

Design Recommendations

1. Enhance Social Navigation: Simplify the process of finding friends and viewing their activity. Create a dedicated "Friends" section on the dashboard where users can easily see what their friends are listening to. Add a search bar within the followers/following sections for quick access to friends' profiles, and allow friend searches through the main search bar.

2. Improve Playlist Management: Move the "Edit" option for playlists to the main screen where users naturally try to rearrange songs, making it more accessible. Introduce a search bar within playlists to allow users to quickly find and reposition songs. Modify the "+" button on the "Now Playing" screen to display a dropdown menu, allowing users to choose whether to add the song to "Liked Songs" or a specific playlist.

3. Consistency Across Platforms: Standardize features across mobile and desktop versions to reduce confusion. Ensure that commonly used features, like playlist editing, are located in the same place on both platforms. Streamline the desktop interface by decluttering and making key features more prominent.

4. Increase Visibility and Feedback for Users: Provide clearer visual cues for interactive elements like the "+" button, indicating what will happen when the user clicks it. Add a tutorial or onboarding process that guides users through finding friends, managing playlists, and other complex tasks.
