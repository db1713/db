PROJECTS

VR Based Stroke Rehabilitation

This project won second prize during my undergraduate program. It focuses on providing rehabilitation for stroke patients using VR technology.

See more

  • Link to the publication↗
  • Stroke patients often require various treatments such as physiotherapy and psychological counseling to regain physical capabilities.
  • Successful therapy can lead to significant improvements in limb mobility for many stroke patients.
  • However, some patients struggle with motivation to attempt limb movements in real environments, which can result in permanent immobility.
  • The pandemic further complicates physical visits to rehabilitation centers, necessitating alternative solutions.
  • Virtual Reality (VR) technology offers a promising solution by providing accessible and affordable rehabilitation processes.
  • A proposed model integrates rehabilitation processes into virtual environments, allowing patients to engage in activities such as tracing movements and stacking boxes using their index fingers.
  • This VR-based approach offers convenience over traditional rehabilitation methods.
  • Quantifying changes in mobility is essential, with measurements against baselines serving as progress indicators.
  • Monitoring progress provides motivation for patients to attempt limb movements, aiding in their rehabilitation journey.
