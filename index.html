VR Based Stroke Rehabilitation

This project won second prize during my undergraduate program. It focuses on providing rehabilitation for stroke patients using VR technology.

See more

  • Link to the publication ↗

Problem Statement

  • Stroke patients often require physiotherapy and psychological counseling to regain physical capabilities.
  • Therapy can lead to significant improvements in limb mobility for many stroke patients.
  • Some patients struggle with motivation to attempt limb movements in real environments, risking permanent immobility.
  • The COVID-19 pandemic complicates physical visits to rehabilitation centers, necessitating alternative solutions.

Solution

  • Virtual Reality (VR) technology offers a promising solution by providing accessible and affordable rehabilitation processes.
  • The proposed model integrates rehabilitation into virtual environments, allowing patients to engage in activities such as tracing movements and stacking boxes using their index fingers.
  • This VR-based approach is more convenient than traditional rehabilitation methods.

Methodology

  • The VR-based system mimics physical therapy activities in a virtual environment.
  • Patients perform exercises like tracing movements and stacking virtual objects using their limbs.
  • The system tracks movements and monitors progress over time.

Results

  • Changes in mobility are quantified and measured against baseline indicators.
  • Progress tracking provides motivation for patients to continue therapy, aiding their rehabilitation journey.

Future Work

  • Integrating AI to adapt exercises based on patient performance.
  • Incorporating additional rehabilitation activities to address more physical challenges for stroke patients.

Mirror Check: VR-Based Driving Test Simulation

A virtual reality simulation to enhance mirror-checking skills during driving tests.

See more

Problem Statement

  • Many new drivers struggle with consistently checking their rearview mirrors, leading to a lack of situational awareness and potential safety hazards.
  • Traditional driving tests often fail to adequately emphasize the importance of mirror-checking in real-time scenarios.

Solution

  • This VR simulation provides an immersive experience where users can practice essential driving skills, particularly focusing on mirror-checking tasks.
  • The system simulates realistic driving scenarios, requiring users to check both internal and external mirrors within a specific time frame.

Methodology

  • Users are tasked with checking both internal and external rearview mirrors in any order within a 5-second window, mimicking real driving conditions.
  • The system evaluates performance based on the user's response time and accuracy, giving immediate feedback on whether they pass or fail the task.

Results

  • The VR simulation improved users' mirror-checking habits, enhancing their situational awareness while driving.
  • The system offers a safe and controlled environment for learners and instructors to practice essential driving maneuvers.

Future Work

  • Incorporating additional driving tasks and situations into the VR system to cover more comprehensive driving skills.
  • Enhancing the simulation with AI-driven feedback to adapt the difficulty of the test based on individual performance.
